Block-Level Elements

Block-level elements occupy the full width of their parent container and create a new line before and after them. This behavior makes them ideal for structuring content into distinct blocks, such as paragraphs, headings, and lists.

  • Paragraph
  • Headings
  • Division


Inline Elements

Inline elements flow along with the surrounding text, occupying only the space required by their content. They do not start on a new line and are commonly used for emphasizing text or embedding content within paragraphs.

  1. Anchor (hyperlink)
  2. Image element
  3. Strong emphasis
